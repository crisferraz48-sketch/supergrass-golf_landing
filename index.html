Good to know before you design your green

Does an artificial green really roll like a natural one?

A properly engineered one does. Ball roll depends on the surface, the sand dressing and — above all — the base underneath. Our greens are built with specialist golf turf over a thick, precisely levelled base, so the ball rolls true and breaks read honestly. The best proof is putting on one: ask us to show you.

What green speed can I have?

The speed is designed, not left to chance — it comes from the combination of surface model and sand load. Our tournament-grade nylon surfaces reach Stimp 10–12, the pace of championship greens. If you prefer something more forgiving for family play, we calibrate it slower.

Can I hit approach shots into it?

Yes — with the right build. We install a shock-absorbing pad beneath the surface (or a nylon surface with the pad integrated) so the green receives wedge shots with realistic check and roll, instead of the ball bouncing through.

How much space do I need?

Less than you'd think. We've built greens from around 12 m² — a serious practice corner — up to 200 m² short-game complexes with fringe, rough, bunkers and multiple hole positions. The design adapts to your garden, not the other way round.

How long does a green last, and what maintenance does it need?

Built properly, a green lasts like any premium installation — our surfaces carry warranties of 10 to 15 years. Maintenance is light but worth doing: periodic brushing and checking the sand dressing keeps the roll consistent. We can take care of it for you.

How much does a custom putting green cost?

It depends entirely on the design: size, surface, base works, and extras like bunkers or approach zones. That's why every project starts with a conversation and a drawn design — then you receive a detailed written quote with no surprises. The first step is free.
